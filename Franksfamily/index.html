Karla Steinberg's tribute 2-23-24
Good morning, everyone.
I'm getting this email to you under the wire, I suppose. Dot, Frank: sorry I haven't been more in touch, or more present around the house the last year. I just wanted to say hello and that I'm thinking of you. I look up at the moon at night when I take Milly out for her last walk. -I don't know why, but I always say hi to Andy when I am out there and I can see the moon. I was at my mother's house last night for dinner. -She's finally gotten used to the "math lamp" that Andy put in so she "wouldn't have to wash dishes in a shadow." And the stove he worked on as a fun pet project still looks great. She keeps his picture on the bookshelf upstairs, and his HAM radio call tag on the marble table in her living room. I have my framed picture of him on my credenza where all my important beloved family members go, and next to his picture are four pebbles from the beach at Cape Cod, where we released his ashes.

Every time I honk my horn I think of Andy, and I apologize to him because I know he absolutely hated the horn. When I chew gum, I'm very cognizant of the fact that he hated being around gum chewers as the chewing was taking place. When I go to Vermont, I remember the story Connie told me about her and Andy driving around with Grandma's ashes; joyriding in the rolling hills of the Green Mountain State. -All this to say, he's very much present in our lives. but I'd still rather have him here, of course.

I think he'd be proud of me for finally socking away the max limit possible into my 401K/Roth IRA. I miss him all the time, especially when I come up with, or come across a particular groan-worthy joke. I hope to get around to 220 Noe when it gets warmer. -I'll bring some sandwiches, and Cel-Ray, too. Thinking of you all.

xoxo,
Karlton(driascissorstinowitzenbergenheimer)

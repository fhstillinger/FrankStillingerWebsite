    PRINCIPAL RESEARCH INTERESTS

    Molecular theory of water and aqueous solutions; phase transitions; glass physics; geometric aspects of packing problems; energy landscape analyses of condensed-matter phenomena; fundamental aspects of quantum chemistry; molecular models for spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry and its application to pre-biotic chemistry.

    PRINCIPAL RESEARCH ACCOMPLISHMENTS

     
    • Development of capillary wave theory for liquid interfaces.
    • Creation of fundamental statistical mechanical theory for water and aqueous solutions, and its implementation in computer simulations.
    • Discovery of the exact Stillinger-Lovett second moment condition for electrolyte distribution functions.
    • First formulation of the "Stillinger cluster" approach to vapor clustering and nucleation.
    • Construction of model potential functions for water and atomic silicon, for use in computer simulation studies.
    • Development of the Lubachevsky-Stillinger algorithm for computer simulation of rigid-particle packings.
    • Mathematical theory for "potential energy landscapes" of condensed-matter phenomena, including introduction of "inherent structures," their basins, and statistical distributions.
    • Discovery and analysis of mathematical singularities that limit 1/Z and Møller-Plesset series convergence for 2-electron ground states.
    • Introduction of the first classical statistical mechanical model for "inverse melting."
    • Basic discoveries concerning hard particle packing geometries, specifically the role of "rattlers," the highest-known ellipsoid packing density, and the proof of stability for "hypostatic" jamming structures of non-spherical particles.
    • Creation of "engineered" isotropic potentials for spontaneous assembly of target structures, including honeycomb, simple cubic, and diamond lattices, as well as structures with negative thermal expansion.
    • Creation and analysis of statistical-mechanical models for spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, relevant to the behavior of molecules in the early terrestrial environment.
